Capstone Project: TerraCare

TerraCare is a soil regeneration consulting service designed to combat soil degradation in regional Australia while achieving both financial and environmental outcomes. The project aligns with UN SDG 15: Life on Land, focusing on sustainable land use and biodiversity renewal.

The business operates through a partnership with Macquarie University, where under the Work Integrated Learning framework, students from engineering, biodiversity, and business contribute to drone testing, soil analysis, and data evaluation. Academic supervision ensures scientific accuracy while lowering research and development costs and creating real-world learning opportunities (Macquarie University, 2024).

As the finance analyst, I developed a five-year investment plan supported by capital budgeting, discounted cash flow, and sensitivity analysis. Using a phased deployment of one million dollars, TerraCare achieved positive cash flow by Year 3 and an after-tax return of 18.9 percent, outperforming the national GDP growth benchmark. This demonstrated that sustainability initiatives can also deliver commercial success.

Advice from the Chief Executive Officer of SoCreative reinforced the need for strategic innovation, while the Deputy Vice Chancellor of Macquarie University highlighted the importance of structured problem solving. Both perspectives influenced my financial approach, ensuring that TerraCare's outcomes remained practical, evidence based, and scalable.
